NeuroPharmacology Laboratory

Advancing neuroscience research through neuroprotection, neurodegenerative disease biology, molecular pharmacology, and translational therapeutic discovery.

Principal Investigator

Leadership and scientific vision

Dr. Justin A

The NeuroPharmacology Laboratory at JSS College of Pharmacy focuses on understanding the molecular mechanisms underlying neurodegenerative disorders and identifying novel therapeutic strategies through pharmacological and translational research.

Our laboratory integrates molecular biology, cell culture, neuropharmacology, microscopy, and natural product research to investigate neuroprotection and disease-modifying approaches.

Research Areas

Current scientific directions

Neurodegenerative Diseases

Investigating ALS, Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson’s disease, and related disorders.

Molecular Pharmacology

Exploring signaling pathways, receptor pharmacology, and therapeutic targets.

Natural Products

Screening plant-derived compounds for neuroprotective and antioxidant properties.

Cellular Neuroscience

Cell culture and fluorescence imaging approaches for disease modeling.

Neuroprotection

Identifying therapeutic agents capable of reducing oxidative stress and neuronal damage.

Translational Research

Bridging laboratory discoveries toward therapeutic development.
